Understand What You're Getting

Quality work means being clear about what actually happens to your car and why each step matters.

Two-Stage Hand Wash

I start with a heavy foam bath using a wash mitt to lift and remove the bulk of the dirt and grime. Then I come back around with a lighter foam application and work through every detail with quality microfiber towels and detailing brushes. I'm talking wheels, grills, decals, trim, door jambs, every corner and crevice. This isn't a quick rinse and go. I'm meticulous about getting into the spots most people skip because those details are what make the difference between a clean car and a properly detailed one.

Clay Bar Decontamination

Over time, your paint collects contaminants that regular washing can't remove. Tree sap, industrial fallout, rail dust, road tar, overspray from construction. It all builds up and bonds to your clear coat. A clay bar treatment physically removes these embedded particles without damaging the paint. You can actually feel the difference when you run your hand across the surface afterward. It goes from textured to glass smooth. This is what prepares your paint for protection and brings back that clean, responsive feel.

Interior Vacuum & Deep Cleaning

Interiors take a beating. Dirt gets tracked in, crumbs fall into crevices, dust settles everywhere. I'm getting into the seams, under the seats, between console gaps, all the places where it builds up. For Protect & Shine, I use a cordless vacuum that handles regular maintenance and cleanup. For Shield & Shine, I bring the heavy duty shop vacuum with serious suction power to pull out everything that's been sitting there.

On top of vacuuming, the shampoo & extractor lifts dirt and grime while simultaneously boosting the color of your fabric and carpet. You'll notice faded seats, carpet, floor mats, headliner and if needed come back to life with a richer, more vibrant look. The shampoo breaks down embedded dirt and oils, and the extractor pulls it all out along with the moisture. This is what removes the smells and stains that vacuuming alone can't touch. Between the deep vacuum work and full shampoo extraction on Shield & Shine, your interior doesn't just look cleaner, it actually is cleaner down to the fibers.

UV Protectant Application

Your dashboard, door panels, leather, and steering wheel take a beating from the sun. I apply a UV protectant that absorbs into the material and blocks damage from UV rays. This finish also restores faded surfaces back to their original color, eliminating that washed-out look sun exposure causes over time. You can choose between a matte or glossy finish depending on your preference. Matte keeps things natural and factory looking. Glossy gives a bit more shine. Either way, it's protecting your interior from cracking, fading, and aging prematurely while bringing back that deep, vibrant appearance.

Ceramic Coating Boost

This isn't a full ceramic coating (those cost $1000+ and take days), but it's a coating boost that adds months of protection in a single application. Water beads off, dirt doesn't bond as easily, and your next wash is way easier. Think of it as a protective layer between your paint and everything trying to mess it up. It won't last forever, but it'll keep your car cleaner longer than wax ever could.
